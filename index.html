  • 1913: Appointed as director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics

  • 1919: The general theory of relativity was confirmed by observations of a solar eclipse, making Einstein world-famous.
  • 1921: Received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Moved to the United States and took a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey, due to the rise of Nazism in Germany.
  • 1939: Wrote a letter to President Roosevelt that led to the Manhattan Project.
  • 1940: Became a U.S. citizen.
  • 1952: Was offered the position of President of Israel but declined.
